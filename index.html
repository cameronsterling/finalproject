Projects

Remote Sensing: Tracking Fires, Conflict, and Agricultural Loss in Syria

This spring, I worked on two remote sensing projects that aimed to figure out the scale of non-routine fire and agricultural loss in Northern Syria.

This project tracks and analyzes Turkish military interventions in Northern Iraq and Syria, focusing on airstrikes and proxies using ACLED data and GIS mapping.

Outdoor Workers in NY

Here, I located where people where working outdoors in New York State, and whether or not the proportion of people working outside had an effect on community health outcomes

Urban Development in Amman: A Lefebvrian Analysis of the Armenian Case

Made possible by the Babel Initiative, I worked with two collegues to conduct field research and write a paper about the effect of Urban Development on the Jordanian-Armenian population's "right to the city." The paper is currently set for publication in CAREP's 4th Edition of Bidaya, an annual journal in the humanities and social sciences. While the final publication will be in French, do not hesitate to reach out to me for an English copy of the publication.
